Timeline

How did we build this in three weeks?

June 6 - June 10

Brainstorming Project Ideas

During the first week of AI Camp we met with our groups and learned about the focus of the camp: computer vision. Computer vision is a field of AI in which the trained model can identify images, videos, and more. After we learned about computer vision and machine learning, we began searching for potential problems a computer vision project could solve. Finally we came to the decision of creating an AI that could identify Honda and Toyota car models to find the average price range of the car or identify stolen cars in the modern world. In order to do this, we also spent some time learning python.

June 13 - June 17

Collecting and Training Data

The second week of camp consisted of more machine learning based topics. We learned about neural networks which are similar to the pathways of the brain and how AI models use them in addition to data labelling technologies like Roboflow and SERP-API in order to identify images. Our team downloaded and uploaded images of Honda and Toyota car models to Roboflow using SERP-API and python and ultimately labelled the data in order for the AI to be able to produce accurate results.

June 20 - June 24

Frontend Development

The last week of camp put all our skills together in a combined effort. We had a trained AI model and created a website using HTML and CSS to present our work. HTML and CSS are partner languages that are both used to manipulate the layout of a website. The final component of a web page is Flask, a python web framework that allowed us to access and put together our website. We used Bootstrap's templates and personalized our website in order for the user to be able to upload a car picture which is on the Detect page.
